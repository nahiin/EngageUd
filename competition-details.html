RWADTHON Competition

Fall 2026  ·  Prince Mohammad bin Fahd University

Event Date June 15, 2026
Location PMU Main Campus, Al Khobar
Prize Pool SAR 30,000

About This Competition

RWADTHON is a flagship innovation hackathon hosted by Prince Mohammad bin Fahd University, designed to bring together the brightest student minds from across the Kingdom to tackle real-world challenges through the power of technology.

Participants form multidisciplinary teams and are challenged to develop, prototype, and present technology-driven solutions within a competitive environment. The competition emphasizes applied thinking, teamwork, and the ability to communicate ideas clearly and persuasively to a panel of expert judges.

Details

RWADTHON 2026 is a 48-hour hackathon open to university and secondary school students nationwide. Teams will work on a problem statement revealed at the start of the event, building functional prototypes or proof-of-concept solutions using any technology stack of their choice.

The competition takes place across two phases:

  • Phase 1 – Online Qualifier: Teams submit a written proposal outlining their proposed solution and its feasibility. Top proposals advance to the main event.
  • Phase 2 – On-Site Hackathon: Selected teams gather at PMU's main campus for the 48-hour build sprint, followed by final presentations to a panel of judges from industry and academia.

All equipment and workspace will be provided. Participants are encouraged to bring their own laptops and any hardware peripherals relevant to their project.

Objectives

RWADTHON is structured around five core objectives that together aim to develop well-rounded innovators capable of thinking critically, building rapidly, and communicating effectively.

  • Foster a culture of innovation, creative problem-solving, and entrepreneurial thinking among Saudi youth.
  • Develop practical, technology-enabled solutions for real-world challenges facing the community and industry.
  • Strengthen collaboration between universities, schools, and the private sector.
  • Provide students with hands-on experience in rapid prototyping, pitching, and cross-disciplinary teamwork.
  • Support Saudi Vision 2030 by nurturing a generation of digitally skilled, innovation-ready graduates.

Evaluation Criteria

Each submission is assessed across four equally weighted criteria by a panel of judges drawn from industry, academia, and the entrepreneurship ecosystem. Teams are evaluated on the overall quality of both their solution and their ability to communicate it.

Innovation & Creativity (25%)
Originality of the idea and the creative approach taken to address the challenge. Solutions that reframe the problem or combine technologies in unexpected ways will score highly here.

Technical Execution (25%)
The quality, functionality, and completeness of the built solution. Judges will look for working prototypes, clean implementation, and evidence that core technical challenges have been handled effectively.

Impact & Feasibility (25%)
The potential to create meaningful, measurable real-world impact, alongside the practicality of bringing the idea to market. Scalability, sustainability, and alignment with Saudi Vision 2030 are considered here.

Presentation (25%)
Clarity, structure, and confidence during the final pitch. Teams should communicate their solution, its value proposition, and their implementation approach in a compelling and concise manner.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Teams must consist of 3 to 5 members. Solo registrations are not accepted.
  • All team members must be currently enrolled students at a recognised university or school.
  • Submitted solutions must be original work developed during the competition period. Pre-built products are not permitted.
  • Each team may only submit one project per competition cycle.
  • The use of open-source libraries and publicly available APIs is allowed, provided they are properly credited.
  • All participants must adhere to PMU's Code of Conduct throughout the event.
  • Judges' decisions are final and no correspondence will be entered into.

Winners

Winners will be announced after the competition closes on May 31, 2026.
