Technology January 15, 2026

RWADTHON 2026 is LIVE — Applications Now Open

Apply now to PMU's most prestigious technology hackathon. Teams of 3–5 students compete for SAR 30,000 in prizes, mentorship, and direct industry exposure.

RWADTHON 2026 is officially open for applications — and this year's edition promises to be the biggest yet. Hosted by Prince Mohammad bin Fahd University's NextGen EngageU Hub in partnership with leading technology firms across the Kingdom, the competition challenges student teams to tackle real, industry-defined problems in a high-energy 48-hour sprint.

This edition introduces a new qualifier round, allowing more teams than ever to get involved. Teams that successfully pass the online proposal stage will be invited to PMU's main campus in Al Khobar for the on-site final, where they'll build, test, and pitch their solutions to a panel of judges drawn from Saudi Aramco, STC, and the Ministry of Communications.

What Makes RWADTHON Different?

Unlike traditional hackathons, RWADTHON is built around real problem statements sourced directly from industry partners. Teams won't be working on abstract challenges — they'll be solving issues that organisations are actively trying to address today. This year's themes include smart infrastructure, digital health, and sustainable logistics.

Mentors from the private sector will be on-site throughout the event, offering technical guidance, business feedback, and networking opportunities. Top-performing teams will also be connected with startup accelerators and investment programmes as part of the Hub's commitment to turning competition outcomes into lasting impact.

Who Can Apply?

  • University students (all years and disciplines)
  • Secondary school students (with faculty supervisor sign-off)
  • Interdisciplinary teams of 3 to 5 members
  • Both Saudi and international participants are welcome

Applications close on May 31, 2026. Teams are encouraged to apply early — qualifying proposals are reviewed on a rolling basis and early applicants receive priority feedback from the organising committee.

"RWADTHON is more than a competition. It's a proving ground — the place where students realise they can build something the world actually needs."

— Dean of Student Affairs, PMU

For full competition details, eligibility requirements, and submission guidelines, visit the official competition page or contact the EngageU Hub team directly at engageu@pmu.edu.sa.
